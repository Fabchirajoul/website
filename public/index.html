INTERNATIONAL PET DELIVERY

International pet travel is vastly different to human travel. There are country regulations, airline stipulations, and veterinary requirements that have to be considered and adhered to before travel arrangements can be made for your pets.

Global Cargo Logistics will provide you with professional and up-to-date international pet transport guidance and expertise concerning your destination country’s specific requirements and your pet’s veterinary preparations. We will happily shoulder all the anxieties you may have to safely and ethically reunite your family in your new home.

We would strongly recommend, that the very first thing you do is to acquaint yourself with these few important points:

Understand your pet’s travel requirements with your consultant’s guidance.

Obtain, receive and accept our quote.

Adhere to your pet’s veterinary schedules and timelines provided by your consultant (unless we are assigned to do it all for you).

You do better when you know better…. Read the below points to Know Better.

Understanding your international pet travel quotation.

Overseas pet travel and animal relocation is often far more expensive than human travel.

Airfreight is calculated on how much space your pet’s travel crate occupies in the airline’s cargo hold. Unfortunately, these prices are never discounted by the airlines. Veterinary fees, airline fuel fees, administration fees, crate charges, and additional customs or state vet endorsements have to be taken into consideration.

We will tailor your quote to suit your needs and requirements. However, if you find that the quote is above your budget please let us know, and perhaps we can advise you on how to reduce costs

Working with a vet.

Indicated below are a few mistakes that vets can make. Please, if you are working with your vet make sure he/she is aware of the following:

Microchips cannot be inserted after the rabies vaccination, unless it is done on the same day.

Do not draw blood for the TITRE rabies blood test less than 30 days after the last rabies vaccination.

Applying for an import permit

An import permit is required for most African, South American & Asian countries. Australia & New Zealand also require an import permit. Countries like The USA, Canada, and most EU countries do not require an import permit.

So, if your destination country requires an import permit please see the notes below:

Import permits have to be obtained from the country of destination.

Import permits need to be applied for far in advance of your pets’ date of departure. It can take several weeks to obtain the permit. It depends on the issuing country..

Every import permit is valid for a certain period of time. This means that your pet will have to travel before the expiry date on the permit.

Every import permit will specify the countries requirements. Read those requirements. This is very important! You have to comply with the countries requirements before your pet’s can travel.

Import permits are valid for one assignment only. This means that every time your pet travels you need to obtain a new import permit from the country of destination.

Sometimes cats and dogs have to travel on separate permits and health certificates.

Generally pets can travel on a copy of the permit as long as the original permit is submitted upon the pet’s arrival.

Departure day.

Our Animal Handler will collect your pets from your residence on the day of their departure (unless kennelling is required prior to travel)..

Do not feed your animals a large meal on the day of departure; a small snack or treat is more suitable. This is because they will be in their travel crate for an extended period and will need to relieve themselves at some point. The larger the meal the greater the chance that your pet will soil their travel crate, this could lead to a very messy dog and travel crate. Alternatively your pet may feel constipated and if they have been fed a large meal this could lead to some discomfort. So please feed them well the night before collection and give your dog a long walk to have enough time for a toilet break before our scheduled collection time.

We all know how intuitive cats can be and they are famous for doing a disappearing act on the day of collection or even the evening before, so please keep your cats securely closed in a room from the evening before scheduled collection so there are no last minute surprises.

Please note that your pets have to travel with their original documentation attached to their travel crate.

Your pets must be booked in, at the airlines, several hours (generally 4 hours) before their time of departure. Our Animal Handler will walk your dog, to let them refresh themselves, just prior to the handover to the airline. Our Animal Handler will also inform you once your pets have been booked in for their flight..

You will be provided with a track and trace website link to check on your pet’s flight status anytime during its journey.

In the case of an emergency we have a 24 hours manned phone line, please find the emergency phone numbers in your consultant’s email stationary.

Your consultant will follow up with you upon your pet’s arrival and again a few days later to check how your furry friend is settling into its new environment..

Arrival at destination..

You can either clear your pets through customs & collect them from the airport yourself OR we can arrange it all and have them delivered to your residence.

Please read further info on both choices below:

Owner clearing and collecting pets themselves after arrival:

The consignee (person collecting the pet) needs to have their ID or passport with them when collecting.

At the destination airport the consignee must go to the airline cargo section at least 40 minutes to an hour after the flight has arrived, show the person at the counter the Air Waybill number that will be supplied, their ID or passport and request their pets’ original documentation..

Then the consignee must go to the customs office with the documentation and obtain customs clearance (you might be expected to pay additional customs fees)..

Once the customs official has stamped the appropriate forms, the consignee must go back to the cargo section, show them the stamped customs forms, pay additional airline handling charges and collect their pets.

This process might be time consuming but should be pretty simple.

Very important: should you be clearing and collecting your pet yourself, please ensure that you contact the cargo section at the airport prior to your pet’s arrival. Please ensure that you are aware of the customs requirements and procedures at the airport where your pet is arriving as we cannot supply you with this information..



Owner clearing and collecting pets themselves after arrival:

Prior to travel your destination travel agent might need information and documentation from you, the owner, i.e. passport copy, visa copy, customs forms that needs to be completed etc.

The destination agent will take care of customs clearance, payment of the airline handling charges and any other import formalities that might need to be done.

After clearance the travel agent could either hand the pet over to you at the airport or deliver to a residence.

Understanding your import pet travel quotation.

Our standard Import Quote usually includes only the entry into the destination country's import services and excludes the airfreight and export services from your country of origin. However, should you require a full door to door service we can happily oblige and provide you with an all inclusive quote.

Quote for Full Export & Import services – Door to Door. …What does this mean?

Your dedicated consultant will advise you on the requirements and documentation needed to enter your country of origin

We will apply for and collect your import permit.

We will appoint a travel agent at your origin and arrange all the veterinary preparations and Export services e.g. schedule for veterinary tests and treatments, endorsement of the health certificate and book flights from your origin to destination country.

Upon arrival in destination country, we will meet the incoming flight and liaise with the state vet and customs officers to clear your pets into your country. We will collect your pet from the airline and deliver to your new residence.

If quarantine is required, we will arrange their stay at the quarantine facility and once completed we will collect (usually a Friday) and deliver your pets to your new residence.

Adhering to the destination country's import requirements.

All Parrots, dogs and cats imported into a country must be registered, as manifest cargo and cannot fly as excess baggage. The Directorate Veterinary Services does not have veterinary officials that can check paperwork at the passenger terminal of departing airport . Dogs, Parrots and cats can only disembark via the cargo terminal of the respective airports.

Cats do not require any blood testing or quarantine in order to enter South Africa.

All dogs arriving into South Africa require blood testing.

Dogs from certain countries require to be quarantined on arrival in the country.

Required documentation for Cats

Import permit – we will obtain it on your behalf. Please note it might take 2-3 working weeks to receive the permit. An Import Permit is valid for 6 months.

Microchip – The microchip must be able to be read by an ISO 11784 or ISO 11785 scanner.

Valid rabies vaccination – must be older than 30 days and younger than 1 year.

Health certificate endorsed by Government Veterinarian within 10 days of export.

Cats do not require any blood testing or quarantine in order to enter into any country

Required documentation for Dogs:

Import permit – we will obtain it on your behalf. Please note it might take 2-3 working weeks to receive the permit. Non-quarantine permit is valid for 6 months.

Microchip – The microchip must be able to be read by an ISO 11784 or ISO 11785 scanners.

Valid rabies vaccination – must be older than 30 days and younger than 1 year

5 Blood tests are required. These blood tests need to be done within 30 days of shipment. Please refer to the paragraph below.

Dogs imported from countries not certified free from Dirofilaria immitis must be treated from the date of negative testing until export at the required intervals as stipulated in the receiving country's health certificate. Sufficient Dirofilaria immitis medication needs to accompany the dog to the receiving country so that the dogs can receive a six month course of medication after importation into the receiving country.

This might not be necessary for import, but will be specified on the health certificate which is issued with the import permit.

The receiving country's Health certificate (including blood test results) must be endorsed by a Government Veterinarian within 10 days of export.

Quarantine is required for dogs only and only from specific countries. Please check with your consultant if quarantine will be applicable to your dogs upon arrival in the receiving country.

Required documentation for Parrots

Import permit – we will obtain it on your behalf. Please note it might take 2-3 working weeks to receive the permit. An Import Permit is valid for 6 months.

Microchip – The microchip must be able to be read by an ISO 11784 or ISO 11785 scanner.

DNA Certificate – must be older than 30 days and younger than 1 year.

Health certificate endorsed by Government Veterinarian within 10 days of export.

Quarantine is required for Parrots only and only from specific countries. Please check with your consultant if quarantine will be applicable to your dogs upon arrival in the receiving country.

CITES for specific birds will be needed. We will obtain it on your behalf. It might take 3 to 4 days to receive the CITES documents. Please ensure your sender has applied for it before coming to us so we can fascilitate the process.

Transfer of owenership: Sender ought to have legally transferred the ownership of the parrots to the consignee.

Parrots do not require any blood testing or quarantine in order to enter into any country

WHY WE ARE DIFERENT




In 1983, Global Cargo Logistics was established with the purpose of providing a low-cost courier service between Napier and Hastings in New Zealand. A year later, Fastway became the first-ever transport company to enter the franchising arena.

In 1993 it was decided to export the business concept to Australia, following a year of research which showed that the same niche market the company had ridden to success in New Zealand, also existed across the Tasman.


After commencing operations in Australia in 1993, Global Cargo Logistics enjoyed phenomenal growth and was announced as the country’s fastest-growing private company in 1998 by BRW Magazine with the growth of 14,557% for the previous five years. The success of Fastway in Australia was immediate, and the upwards growth trend continues to this day.

Global Cargo Logistics later embarked on an expansion program, offering a National Master Franchise to suitable candidates, to operate the Fastway business for an entire country.


GLobal Cargo Logistics commenced operation in South Africa in 2008 and now has 15 regional franchises across the country.

In 2011, the Global Cargo Logistics brand began a major revitalisation; an exciting change to the way we look and a renewed focus on our valued customers. Underpinning that is our key value system of delighting the customer at every point in the parcel’s journey. And in 2014 Fastway completed the brand refresh in South Africa.


Our new look is designed to reflect our growth, progression and of course, our overwhelming ambition to be one of the leading courier companies in operation today.


Today, we’re proud to be able to offer a low cost, quality courier service to customers, large and small across South Africa, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland and Northern Ireland.

What if I'm unable to sign for the delivery? If you're unable to sign for your parcel, we're able to leave the parcel in a secure place at your address without a signature. 1. Why would I complete an Authority to leave form?
You might decide to complete an Authority to leave form if you're unable to sign for your parcel. For example, you might be at work.

2. Who’s liable if the parcel gets lost, stolen or damaged?
When you complete an Authority to leave form, Fastway Global Cargo Logistics are not liable for any loss or damage to the parcel. On the form, you should specify a secure place for your parcel to be left.

3. How do I have all my parcels delivered without a signature?
If you'd like all future parcels to be left without a signature, please contact your nearest depot. We'll send you the form to complete - hand it to your Courier Franchisee, or email it to your local depot.
When you order your items online, make sure you include clear instructions to leave it without a signature.

What if my parcel arrives damaged? If your parcel arrives damaged, you should contact the sender and let them know and they’ll advise you of the next steps.

How should I package my item? We know how important it is that your parcel arrives safely and undamaged.

1. Choose a good quality box
A double walled box, especially for deliveries that contain fragile items, secured appropriately is best.

2. Choose the right sized box
Boxes that are too small could mean the contents get lost. Boxes that are too large are likely to collapse if another parcel is placed on top of it during delivery.

3. Protect your items
Bubble wrap, packing peanuts, foam, and newspaper can all be used to protect your items.

4. Wrap each item in the box individually
When sending multiple items within one box it’s recommended that each item is wrapped individually. This offers better protection for your items and reduces movement.

5. Seal the parcel with packing tape
Seal all open edges of the parcel with wide parcel tape on both the top and bottom of your package to ensure it doesn’t open during transit.

6. Labelling your parcel
Always use clear, accurate address labels on your parcels.
